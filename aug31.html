LETTER FROM THE EDITOR
INTERVIEW WITH QUENTIN LEBEAU BY LYRIC LEWIN


As Whetstone Media grows, we want our readers to feel connected with the team behind the brand. With that in mind, we wanted to take some time to introduce the people behind the scenes.

Name: Quentin Lebeau
Location: Seattle, WA
Associate Producer + Executive Assistant



As Origin Foragers at Whetstone Media, we want to honor all origins. To that end, what are your origins?

In all honesty, I am pretty unfamiliar with my origins. I know that my family comes from a French/Acadian/Cajun background, but past that I don’t know much. My French-speaking grandparents opted to not pass down their language or traditions, and as a result, there isn’t much knowledge in terms of origins.

What do your origins mean to you?

For me, my origins have been about working backward to uncover my family history, rather than having [it] passed down. This curiosity actually led me to study abroad in Nantes, France—the area where my family had first come from.



What is a dish that meant a lot to you growing up?

Crêpes are something that meant a lot to me. They were usually a Sunday morning treat, and definitely a comfort food. They weren’t only really good, but also provided some kind of connection for me to my family’s past. It made them feel special and personal.

What is a dish you create to share with others?

Crêpes are actually also something I really enjoy sharing. They’re fun and easy to make, and so customizable. You can bring in different ingredients from totally different places and contexts to make something unique. I think this aspect makes it such a great food to share.

When did you start working at Whetstone?

I started with Whetstone in 2019 as an intern while I was still in college, and then after graduation, I was able to take on a bigger role and go full time.

What drew you to working in food media?

As someone interested in stories, and connections, I like that food is both so personal and so universal. It is something that everyone can share in, but that everyone has a unique and singular connection to. Food serves as a great bridge to learning about so many other things in people’s lives, history and culture.
