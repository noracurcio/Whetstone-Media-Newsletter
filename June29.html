DIASPORA CO. AND
WHETSTONE SOUTH ASIA COLLAB

LETTER FROM THE EDITOR, LYRIC LEWIN
WITH SOUTH ASIA EDITOR, VIDYA BALACHANDER

Diaspora Co.'s pioneering commitment to decolonize the spice trade and source high-quality, single-origin spices needs no introduction. Given Whetstone's similar commitment to arrive at the heart of food origins, a partnership seemed like the best showcase for our shared ideals. The Diaspora Co. x Whetstone partnership will introduce readers to the origins of spices and illustrate why history and context remains so critical to any understanding of the spice trade. (Along with the serious stories, we also hope to have some fun!) Stay tuned for original, insightful, and engaging spice stories coming your way.



Vidya, you have helmed the South Asia section for Whetstone Magazine - the very first foray into a regional coverage vertical for the company. Can you tell us about the stories you choose to feature, and what matters most to you in good storytelling?

Vidya: To some extent, I think the groundwork for the vertical had already been laid even before I took over. The sheer volume of pitches Whetstone received from South Asia meant that we had a diversity of voices to showcase. As editor of Whetstone's South Asia vertical, I consider it my role to curate that selection of voices and stories to reflect the true complexity of South Asian cuisines, cultures, and lived experiences, free from the often reductive (or at least limited) lens that they are seen through in Western food media. To this extent, the stories I tend to gravitate towards are those that have layers of complexity — or those that are willing to push the narrative in surprising ways. At the heart of it, food is both personal and political, and stories that are willing to dig deeper are the ones that I am most excited by.

Can you give us any insight into the stories you're featuring about the history of Nutmeg, Chai, and other spices or foods special/particular to the region?

Vidya:As Sana (Sana Javeri Kadri - founder of Diaspora Co.) has so powerfully showcased through her work, the history of spices is complicated. After all, wars were fought and countries colonized in pursuit of the riches from the spice trade! This new collaboration attempts to introduce readers to some of that complicated context. For instance, Sharanya Deepak's story on Masala chai revisits the history of how this drink came into being and also places it in a contemporary context, asserting that no two cups of chai — both in terms of their composition and cultural weight — can be seen as the same. The nutmeg and cacao pieces dwell on the problematic historical aspects of the spice trade, as a reminder that the question of ethical sourcing has always been a weighty and important one.



Can you share with us how the partnership with Diaspora Co. fits into your coverage, and what you hope people take away or learn?

Vidya:I think this collaboration is, in many ways, an extension of what we aspire to do at Whetstone — tracing origins, delving into food history, and providing essential context and subtext to our understanding of food and why it matters. In that sense, I think readers of Whetstone would find plenty to their interest in this new collaboration.



JESSAMINE STARR'S FRUIT LOVE LETTERS PODCAST

Fruit expert and lover, Jessamine Starr, @jessaminestarr, host of Fruit Love Letters, @fruitloveletters, shows us how to make blueberry coriander jam!
RECIPE
2 cups blueberries
1 tablespoon coriander
1/4 cup sugar
2 tablespoons chia seeds
Juice of 1/2 a lemon

First toast coriander seeds on the stovetop until fragrant. Lightly crush in a spice grinder, or smash the seeds with the back of a knife. Keeping some texture of the coriander is good. Combine the crushed coriander with the blueberries, sugar, chia seeds, and lemon juice in a pot and cook on medium high heat till juicy and jam-like. This should take about 5 minutes. More thickening will occur as the jam cools. Store the jam in the fridge for up to 1 week.

Enjoy it on buttered toast with a higher jam > than toast ratio!
